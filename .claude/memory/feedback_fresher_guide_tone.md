No "15 years experience" or "mentor says" framing — speak directly to students, peer-to-peer.

**Why:** User explicitly asked to remove senior/mentor framing. Content should feel like honest, natural guidance — not authority lecturing.

**How to apply:**
- Hero badges: "YOUR PATH — YOUR CHOICE" not "FROM A SENIOR DEVELOPER"
- Section intros: "Here is what is true" not "I have seen hundreds of students..."
- Final advice: action steps, not mentor wisdom quotes
- Tone: direct, motivating, honest — like a smart friend who knows the industry

**Content rules per role:**
- Always include passion/interest dimension (who it genuinely fits)
- Always include AI impact (what AI is doing, what it cannot do)
- Always include future outlook
- Acknowledge hard realities (hiring slowdown, AI threat) — do not soften for comfort
- Encourage researching company-specific needs (LinkedIn job postings)
- Warn about choosing paths without genuine interest
